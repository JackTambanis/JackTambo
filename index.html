STATIC ECHOES

Long exposure, almost still. City reflections held at the edge of motion, like a thought you almost said out loud.

DIGITAL FLORA

Screens photographed like petals, pixels blooming into non‑existent flowers. A garden grown from error messages.

NIGHT RESIDUE

Sodium lights, wet pavement, distant music. The afterimage of a walk home long after you forget the route.

ABOUT

Jack Tambo

Jack works in the space between clarity and error: long exposures, reflections, improvised lenses, and the kind of motion blur you usually try to avoid.

The images are printed large, often on matte paper, to soften the digital edges. Online, the work is presented in slow layers: black and white type, restrained motion, colour only where the photograph insists.

This site is a quiet counterpart to the prints – an unfolding sequence of frames instead of a grid, closer to listening to an album than scrolling a feed.
