About Jack

Jack Tambo is an abstract photographer who treats cameras like instruments – not to record reality, but to mis-hear it.

The work on this site comes from long exposures, reflections in broken glass, improvised lenses, and a refusal to accept that light has to behave. Every photograph is an experiment where accidents are invited and carefully amplified.

Instead of traditional categories like “landscape” or “portrait”, Jack organises images into loose emotional circuits: echoes, static, caustics, spectral traces. The gallery reshuffles itself to mirror the way memory misfiles moments.

Move slowly. Hover. Drag. The more you interact, the more the layout reveals tiny behaviours – micro-glitches, tilts, breathing shadows – that are easy to miss at first glance.
